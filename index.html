Gai Jatra Festival

A Traditional Festival of Nepal

About

Gai Jatra, the "Festival of Cows," is celebrated mainly in the Kathmandu Valley. Families who lost loved ones in the past year join processions with decorated cows or children dressed as cows. The festival blends remembrance with joy, humor, and culture.

History

Originating in the Malla period, King Pratap Malla introduced the festival to comfort his queen after the loss of their son. He encouraged families to come together, showing that death is universal and part of life.

Celebration

  • Processions with cows or children in cow costumes.
  • Colorful costumes, dances, and cultural performances.
  • Comedy, satire, and songs that freely express opinions.
